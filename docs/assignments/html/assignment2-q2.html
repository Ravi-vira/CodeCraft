                <section>
                    <h2>Ingredients</h2>
                    <p>Gather the following ingredients to prepare approximately 20 Jalebis:</p>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>1 cup</strong> all-purpose flour (maida)</li>
                        <li><strong>1 tbsp</strong> chickpea flour (besan)</li>
                        <li><strong>1/2 tsp</strong> baking powder</li>
                        <li><strong>3/4 cup</strong> plain yogurt</li>
                        <li><strong>1/4 tsp</strong> turmeric powder (for color)</li>
                        <li><strong>2 cups</strong> sugar</li>
                        <li><strong>1 cup</strong> water</li>
                        <li>A few strands of saffron (optional)</li>
                        <li>Oil or ghee for frying</li>
                    </ul>
                </section>

                <section>
                    <h2>Steps</h2>
                    <p>Follow these steps to prepare crispy and juicy <em>Gujarati Jalebis</em>:</p>
                    <ol>
                        <li>In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, chickpea flour, and baking powder.</li>
                        <li>Add yogurt and a little water to create a smooth, thick batter. Add turmeric for a rich color.</li>
                        <li>Cover the batter and let it ferment for <strong>6â€“8 hours</strong> (preferably overnight).</li>
                        <li>Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until it forms a single-thread consistency. Add saffron for enhanced flavor.</li>
                        <li>Heat oil or ghee in a pan for deep frying.</li>
                        <li>Pour the batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle with a small nozzle.</li>
                        <li>Squeeze the batter in circular motions into the hot oil, forming spirals.</li>
                        <li>Fry until golden and crisp, then immediately dip in warm sugar syrup for a few seconds.</li>
                        <li>Remove and let the Jalebis rest for a minute before serving.</li>
                    </ol>
                </section>

                <section>
                    <h3>Tips for Best Results</h3>
                    <p>Ensure the batter is of <em>pouring consistency</em>, not too thick or thin.</p>
                    <p>Fermentation is the key to perfect Jalebis. Allow the batter to rest for at least 6 hours, ideally overnight.</p>
                    <p>For extra flavor, soak the Jalebis in syrup on the 2<sup>nd</sup> day for an even richer taste.</p>
